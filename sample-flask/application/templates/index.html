The Sun - Our Dazzling Star

The Sun is the star at the center of the Solar System. It is a nearly perfect sphere of hot plasma, with internal convective motion that generates a magnetic field via a dynamo process.

The Planets - A Diverse Family

The Solar System consists of eight planets: Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune. Each planet has unique characteristics and offers a fascinating glimpse into the diversity of our celestial neighbors.

Dwarf Planets and Other Celestial Objects

In addition to the planets, the Solar System is also home to several dwarf planets such as Pluto, Ceres, Haumea, Makemake, and Eris. These objects share similarities with planets but have not cleared their orbits.

Moons - Worlds in Orbit

Many celestial bodies in the Solar System have moons orbiting around them. For example, Earth has one moon, while Jupiter, the largest planet, has 79.
